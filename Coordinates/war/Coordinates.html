Bases and Coordinates

Blue and green lines are basis vectors; the red lines are a vector that is shared across all pictures. Grab the head of a vector in the picture on the left with your mouse to move it.
The pictures correspond to a basis we call S. The picture on the left shows what the coordinate system for S looks like from the standard perspective on R^2. The picture on the right shows what the standard R^2 axes loook like from the perspective of S.
There is a shared vector, v, that appears in red in both of the pictures. Below the pictures, there is a description of v as a coordinate vector in S, [v]_S.
