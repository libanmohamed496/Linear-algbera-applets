Coordinates and Transition Matrices

Blue and green lines are basis vectors; the red lines are a vector that is shared across all pictures. Grab the head of a vector in either of the top two pictures with your mouse to move it.
The pictures on the left correspond to a basis we call S; the pictures on the right correspond to a basis we call T. For each basis, the picture on top shows what the coordinate system for that basis looks like from the standard perspective on R^2. The bottom pictures show what the standard R^2 axes loook like from the perspective of the non-standard basis.
There is a shared vector, v, that appears in red in each of the pictures. Below the pictures, there is a description of v as a coordinate vector in each basis and the transition matrix needed to convert the coordinate vector for v in terms of S to the coordinate vector for v in terms of T.
[v]_S refers to the coordinate vector for V in terms of S. [v]_T refers to the coordinate vector for v in terms of T. P_TS refers to the matrix that converts [v]_S into [v]_T via matrix multiplication.
